Getting your music out to the world has never been more accessible — or more competitive. In 2026, independent artists have more tools than ever, but standing out requires strategy. This guide covers everything you need to know.

## What is Music Distribution?

Music distribution is the process of delivering your music to streaming platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, Tidal, YouTube Music, and 150+ others. Unlike the old days of physical CDs, digital distribution means your music can reach every corner of the world instantly.

## How Distribution Works

1. You upload your music and artwork to a distributor
2. The distributor delivers it to all platforms
3. When someone streams your song, the platform pays the distributor
4. The distributor pays you (minus their fee)

## Choosing the Right Distributor

There are dozens of distributors in 2026. Here's what to look for:

### Key Factors
- **Royalty percentage** — some take a cut, others charge a flat fee
- **Platform reach** — how many stores they distribute to
- **Speed** — how fast your music goes live
- **Analytics** — the quality of their reporting dashboard
- **Additional features** — playlist pitching, sync licensing, social media monetisation

### Top Distributors in 2026
- **DistroKid** — flat annual fee, unlimited releases, fast delivery
- **TuneCore** — per-release pricing, strong analytics
- **CD Baby** — one-time fee per release, good for physical too
- **Amuse** — free tier available, great for new artists

## Getting on Spotify

Spotify is still the world's largest streaming platform in 2026. Here's how to maximise your presence:

### Claim Your Spotify for Artists Profile
Immediately after your first release, claim your artist profile. This gives you access to:
- Pitch songs to Spotify's editorial playlists
- See real-time streaming stats
- Update your profile photo and bio
- Announce upcoming releases

### Playlist Strategy
Editorial playlists are gold — but algorithmic playlists are where most artists build momentum. Focus on:

1. **Release Radar** — Spotify's weekly personalised playlist. Getting saves on release day pushes you here
2. **Discover Weekly** — based on listening patterns of your existing fans
3. **Genre playlists** — pitch to independent playlist curators in your niche

### The First 7 Days Matter Most
Spotify's algorithm judges your release heavily in the first week. On release day:
- Share everywhere simultaneously
- Ask fans to save the song (not just stream it)
- Run a small ad campaign to drive traffic
- Pitch to playlist curators 4 weeks before release

## Apple Music

Apple Music pays higher per-stream rates than Spotify. Don't neglect it. Claim your Apple Music for Artists profile and submit your music for editorial consideration through your distributor.

## TikTok & Social Platforms

In 2026, TikTok remains one of the most powerful discovery engines for music. A single viral video can generate hundreds of thousands of streams overnight.

### TikTok Strategy
- Create short, engaging clips using your most memorable hook
- The first 3 seconds must grab attention
- Encourage user-generated content (challenges, duets)
- Post consistently — the algorithm rewards frequency

### Instagram Reels & YouTube Shorts
Cross-post your TikTok content to Reels and Shorts. Each platform's algorithm can independently surface your music to new audiences.

## Promotion Strategy

Distribution without promotion is like opening a shop with no sign outside.

### Organic Promotion
- Build a genuine fanbase through consistent content
- Share behind-the-scenes of your creative process
- Engage with fans personally — reply to comments
- Collaborate with other artists in your genre

### Paid Promotion
- Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — highly targeted by genre, age, location
- Spotify Ad Studio — promote directly within Spotify
- YouTube Ads — video pre-rolls for music videos
